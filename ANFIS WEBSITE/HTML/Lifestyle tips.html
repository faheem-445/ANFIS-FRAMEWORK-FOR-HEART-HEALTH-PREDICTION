2. Stay Active but Take It Slow

  • Start with light activities like walking and increase gradually.
  • Follow your cardiac rehab program if prescribed.
  • Avoid heavy lifting or strenuous activities until approved by your doctor.

3. Manage Medications Properly

  • Take prescribed medications exactly as directed.
  • Never skip doses and always consult your doctor before stopping any medication.
  • Keep a medication tracker to stay organized.

4. Maintain a Healthy Weight

  • Avoid rapid weight gain, which can strain your heart.
  • Track weight regularly to detect any sudden changes.

8. Attend All Follow-Up Appointments

  • Regular check-ups help detect complications early.
  • Report symptoms like chest pain, swelling, or shortness of breath immediately.

9. Take Care of Your Incision

  • Keep the surgical site clean and dry.
  • Watch for signs of infection (redness, swelling, drainage).

10. Stay Positive & Seek Support

  • Join a cardiac rehabilitation program for guidance.
  • Talk to family, friends, or support groups for emotional well-being.
