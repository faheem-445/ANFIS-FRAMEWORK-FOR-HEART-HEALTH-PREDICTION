Heart Disease Treatment

Lifestyle Changes

A) Diet & Nutrition

Heart-Healthy Diet : Emphasizes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.

Increased Omega-3 Fatty Acids : Found in fish, flaxseeds, and walnuts, supports heart health.

B) Physical Activity

Moderate Exercise (30 mins/day, 5x per week) : Walking, cycling, swimming to improve heart function.

Cardiac Rehabilitation : Supervised exercise programs for patients recovering from heart conditions.

C) Stress Management

Meditation, Yoga, & Deep Breathing : Helps reduce cortisol levels.

Counseling & Therapy : Can help manage stress-related heart issues.

Medications

A) Medications for Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

Beta-Blockers (e.g., Metoprolol, Atenolol) : Reduce heart rate and blood pressure.

ACE Inhibitors (e.g., Lisinopril, Enalapril) : Lower blood pressure and prevent heart damage.

B) Medications for Arrhythmias

Antiarrhythmics (e.g., Amiodarone, Flecainide) : Control abnormal heart rhythms.

Anticoagulants (e.g., Warfarin, Apixaban) : Prevent stroke in atrial fibrillation.

C) Medications for Hypertension & Hypertensive Heart Disease

ACE Inhibitors & ARBs (e.g., Losartan, Ramipril) : Control blood pressure.

Beta-Blockers : Slow heart rate and reduce pressure.

Medical Procedures

A) Treatments for Heart Failure

Heart Valve Repair/Replacement : For damaged heart valves (e.g., mechanical or biological valves).

Heart Transplant : For end-stage heart failure when no other treatments work.

B) Treatments for Valvular Heart Disease

Balloon Valvuloplasty : Uses a balloon to widen narrowed valves.

Valve Repair or Replacement Surgery : Fixes or replaces malfunctioning heart valves.

C) Treatments for Congenital Heart Defects

Catheter-Based Procedures : Fixes small holes (e.g., atrial septal defect closure).

Open-Heart Surgery : For complex congenital heart defects.

Emergency Care

A) Treatments for Aortic Aneurysm & Pericarditis

Aneurysm Repair Surgery : Grafting or stenting to prevent rupture.

Pericardiocentesis : Draining excess fluid around the heart.

B) Cardiac Arrest (Sudden Cardiac Arrest - SCA)

Perform CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) : Start chest compressions (100-120 per minute) and rescue breaths.

Use an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) : If available, apply defibrillator pads and follow instructions.

C) Pericardial Tamponade

Pericardiocentesis : A needle is inserted to drain excess fluid from the heart.

Emergency Surgery : If pericardiocentesis is ineffective.
