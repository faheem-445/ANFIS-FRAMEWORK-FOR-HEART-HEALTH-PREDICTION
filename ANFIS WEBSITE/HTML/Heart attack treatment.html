Heart Attack Treatment

Emergency Response

In the event of a heart attack, quick action can save a life. Emergency treatment includes:

A) First Aid & Immediate Actions

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) : If the person is unconscious and not breathing, CPR can help maintain circulation until emergency responders arrive.

Use of an AED (Automated External Defibrillator) : If available, an AED can be used to restore a normal heart rhythm in case of cardiac arrest.

B) Nitroglycerin

Pain Relief & Blood Flow Improvement : Helps dilate coronary arteries, improving blood flow to the heart.

C) Morphine

For Severe Pain : Used in hospital settings to relieve severe chest pain.

Medical Treatments

Once in the hospital, several treatments may be used based on severity:

A) Clot-Busting Medications

Thrombolytics :Used if an angioplasty is not immediately available.

Examples : Alteplase (tPA), Reteplase, Streptokinase.

B)Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI)

Procedure : A catheter is inserted into the blocked artery.

When used : Preferred within 90 minutes of arrival at the hospital.

C) ACE Inhibitors & ARBs

Angiotensin II Receptor Blockers : Help lower blood pressure and reduce heart strain.

Examples :Lisinopril, Losartan.

Recovery & Lifestyle Changes

After the initial treatment, long-term management focuses on recovery and preventing another heart attack.

A) Cardiac Rehabilitation

Supervised Exercise Program : Helps restore heart function safely.

Emotional Support & Stress Management : Anxiety and depression are common after a heart attack.

B)Lifestyle Changes

Dietary Changes : Reduce saturated fats, trans fats, and cholesterol.

Quit Smoking & Alcohol Moderation :Smoking cessation reduces the risk of future heart attacks.

Weight Management : Maintain a healthy weight to reduce strain on the heart.
