ANFIS

ANFIS (Adaptive Neuro-Fuzzy Inference System) is a powerful hybrid modeling approach that combines the strengths of neural networks and fuzzy logic principles. It is designed to model complex, nonlinear systems by learning from data and providing interpretable results through fuzzy inference.

Neural Networks

ANFIS uses neural network learning techniques to automatically adjust the parameters of fuzzy inference systems. This enables the system to learn from data, optimizing its performance over time.

Fuzzy Logic

Fuzzy inference systems use linguistic rules and membership functions to handle uncertainty and approximate reasoning. ANFIS leverages fuzzy logic to make decisions based on imprecise inputs, similar to human reasoning.

Sugeno-Type FIS

ANFIS typically employs a Sugeno-type FIS, where the output of each rule is a linear function of the input variables. This structure makes it suitable for adaptive learning and fine-tuning of parameters.

Gradient Descent (Backpropagation)

Used to adjust the parameters of the membership functions, minimizing the error between the predicted and actual outputs.

Least Squares Estimation (LSE)

Used to optimize the linear parameters of the output functions in the Sugeno-type FIS. This step minimizes the overall prediction error.

Training and Adaptation

ANFIS learns by iteratively adjusting its parameters through training epochs. Each epoch involves a forward pass (where inputs are processed and outputs are calculated) and a backward pass (where errors are used to update the parameters)

Heart Diseases

Heart diseases encompass a range of conditions that affect the heart's structure and function.

These conditions can be congenital (present at birth) or acquired due to lifestyle factors, other health conditions, or genetic predispositions. Heart disease is one of the leading causes of death worldwide, affecting millions of people each year. Early detection and management are crucial to preventing severe complications.

  • Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)
  • Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack)
  • Heart Failure
  • Arrhythmias
  • Valvular Heart Disease
  • Congenital Heart Defects
  • Hypertensive Heart Disease

Heart Attack

A heart attack, medically known as a myocardial infarction, occurs when the blood flow to a part of the heart muscle is blocked, usually due to a buildup of fatty deposits (plaque) in the coronary arteries.

Plaque Rupture

When a plaque ruptures, a blood clot forms, obstructing the artery.

Spasm of a Coronary Artery

Rarely, a coronary artery can spasm and restrict blood flow.

Medical Conditions

High blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, obesity.

Chest Pain or Discomfort

Often described as a squeezing, fullness, or pain in the center or left side of the chest.

Electrocardiogram (ECG)

Measures the electrical activity of the heart to detect heart attacks.

Surgical Procedures

Coronary angioplasty and stenting, coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG).
